## Compression Algorithms — Complete Reference

### Lossless: Statistical Methods
- **Huffman coding**: Builds binary tree from symbol frequencies, assigns prefix-free variable-length codes. Optimal among symbol-by-symbol codes but wastes bits when probabilities aren't powers of 1/2. Canonical Huffman stores only code lengths (used in DEFLATE). [inferred]
- **Arithmetic coding**: Encodes entire message as single fraction in [0,1). Achieves compression arbitrarily close to entropy. Uses fixed-precision integer arithmetic in practice. Used in JPEG 2000, H.264/H.265 CABAC. [inferred]
- **ANS (Asymmetric Numeral Systems)**: Jarek Duda, 2009-2014. Arithmetic-coding ratios at Huffman-like speed. Two variants: rANS (integer state) and tANS (lookup tables). Patent-free. LIFO encoding order. Used in Zstandard and Apple LZFSE. [inferred]

### Lossless: Dictionary Methods
- **LZ77**: Sliding window (32-64KB), emits (distance, length) back-references or literals. Foundation of most modern compressors. Decompression is fast (just copy bytes). [inferred]
- **LZ78**: Explicit dictionary built during encoding. Entries numbered sequentially. Generally worse than LZ77 variants. [inferred]
- **LZW**: Refinement of LZ78 — emits only dictionary indices. Decoder reconstructs dictionary in lockstep. Used in GIF, caused patent controversy. [inferred]

### Lossless: Transform Methods
- **RLE**: Replaces runs with count+symbol. O(n) time. Often a preprocessing step. [inferred]
- **BWT (Burrows-Wheeler Transform)**: Reversible transform that groups characters by following context. Creates runs of similar chars, ideal for RLE/MTF. Used in bzip2. [inferred]
- **Delta encoding**: Stores differences between sequential values. Used in git packfiles, time series, FLAC. [inferred]

### Lossless: Modern Compressors (Hybrid)
- **DEFLATE**: LZ77 + canonical Huffman. 32KB window. Three block types (stored, fixed, dynamic). Most widely used algorithm in history (gzip, ZIP, PNG, HTTP). [inferred]
- **Brotli (Google, 2013)**: 2nd-order context modeling, 16MB window, 120KB static dictionary of web strings. 20-26% smaller than gzip on web content. RFC 7932. [inferred]
- **Zstandard (Facebook, 2015-2016)**: LZ77 + FSE (tANS entropy coder). Up to 128MB window. Dictionary training for small records. At default level: gzip-9 ratio, 5-10x faster. Used in Linux kernel, Chrome. [inferred]

### Lossy: Image
- **JPEG (1992)**: RGB→YCbCr, chroma subsampling, 8x8 DCT, quantization (the lossy step), zigzag scan + RLE + Huffman. Blockiness artifacts at low quality. [inferred]
- **JPEG 2000**: Full-image DWT (wavelets), EBCOT arithmetic coding. No block artifacts, progressive decoding, supports lossless mode. Used in digital cinema, medical imaging. Failed to displace JPEG due to complexity/patents. [inferred]

### Lossy: Audio
- **MP3 (1993)**: Polyphase filter bank → MDCT, psychoacoustic model (frequency masking, temporal masking), bit allocation, quantization + Huffman. 128 kbps = ~10:1 compression. [inferred]
- **AAC (1997)**: Pure MDCT, TNS, perceptual noise substitution, SBR for low bitrates. Better than MP3 at all bitrates, especially below 128 kbps. Used in iTunes, YouTube. [inferred]
- **Opus (2012)**: Hybrid SILK (speech) + CELT (music), seamlessly blends based on content. Surpasses MP3/AAC at all bitrates. 5ms latency. IETF RFC 6716. Used in WebRTC, Discord, WhatsApp. [inferred]

### Lossy: Video
- **H.264/AVC (2003)**: I/P/B frames, motion estimation (16x16 to 4x4 blocks), 4x4 integer DCT, CAVLC or CABAC, in-loop deblocking. Most deployed video codec ever. 50:1 to 200:1 compression. [inferred]
- **H.265/HEVC (2013)**: CTUs up to 64x64 with quad-tree partitioning, 35 intra prediction directions, CABAC only, SAO filter. ~50% bitrate reduction vs H.264. Adoption slowed by patent complexity. [inferred]
- **AV1 (2018)**: Open/royalty-free. 128x128 superblocks, 56 directional modes, CDEF, loop restoration, film grain synthesis, ANS entropy coding. ~30% better than HEVC. 5-20x slower encoding. Used in YouTube, Netflix. [inferred]
